# Blockchain

Blockchain is:
- a decentralized & secure ledger.
- a chain of blocks that securely stores data about transactions and events that happened within one blockchain network.
- a database / collection of data that is decentralized(everyone stores a copy and can read to and write from it).

Although a blockchain is different from a traditional database because:
- data is not stored in one central location.
- a record of the ledger is stored amongst partipicants and they can verify all of the data that is being added.
- there is no single administrator & everyone has administrator privileges.

Having multiple copies of the database among partipicants and decentralized authority describes the concept of "**the distributed ledger technology**" which blockchain is built on. A distributed ledger is just a database spread across multiple locations or partipicants. Blokchain builds on top of that by implementing a data structure as a chain of blocks and enforces a secure way to make and verify transactions. 

## Blockchain Networks
Blockchain transactions can be public or private and have many partipicants from around the world who do not know or trust one another but still work together. This is possible because there are consensus algorithms that define how to come to an agreement and make transactions in a safe and easy way. 

Transactions between partipicants are secure and require that they are verified before adding new blocks to the blockchain. In addition, all transaction are linked in the blockchain and provide full transparency by allowing partipicants to view transactions from beginning of the blockchain history. 

## History of Blockchain
Blockchain was created by two researchers who wanted a way to create a timestamp system that was reliable and cannot be tampered with. Others than built off this idea and explored the currency system that would allow transactions between parties in a secure way. [The first transaction involving physical goods was paid on the 22nd May 2010, by transacting 10 000 BTC in exchange two pizzas to be delivered by Papa John's.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_bitcoin)

## Bitcoin

Bitcoin is a system where everyone can partipicate transactions happened securely, partipicants are encouraged to get involved by trading and where transactions that get added to the blocks in the blockchain are all verified by other partipicants and built off of blockchain technology.

## How Blockchain has Evolved

Blockchain 1.0 is all about cryptocurrency. This includes cryptocurrency blockchains like Bitcoin, Ethereum which are most popular. Since the popularity of Bitcoin grew, we have seen a flux of cryptocurrencies emerge as a result. Blockchain 2.0 is about **smart contracts**. For cryptocurrencies like Ethereum, you can program agreements or contracts between parties. 3.0 is decentralized applications or dApps for short. dApps are built around smart contracts and create an interactable interface. Blockchain 4.0 will be all about making blockchain usable in multiple industries.
