# Types of Blockchain

There are four types of a blockchain. These are:
- Public 
- Private
- Hybrid
- Consortium/Federated

## Public Blockchains

Public blockchains are permissionless, completely decentralized [distributed ledger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distributed_ledger) systems where anyone can join and partipicate, do transactions and be a part of the blockchain. All nodes have equal rights for accessing the blockchain, creating new blocks of data and validating blocks of data & ther is no central authority. Transactions are anonymous but transparent to everyone.

Public blockchains are mostly used for cryptocurrencies. There are popular public blockchains such as Bitcoin, Ethereum & Litecoin. On these public blockchains, the nodes **mine** for creating blocks by solving cryptographic equations. The miner nodes earn small amount of fee for their efforts.

Advantages of public blokchains are independency, transparency and trust. On the other hands disadvantages of public blockchains are security, scalability and performance. Most popular use cases of public blokchains are cryptocurrency, voting, fundraising & document validation. 

## Private Blockchains

 Private blockchains are permissioned blockchains controlled by an organisation or a group. In private blockchains, a central authority determines who can be the node of the blockchain.
 
 The central authority does not grant each node with equal rights. Public access to private blockchains is restricted so private blockchains are not entirely decentralized. There are examples of private blockchains such as Ripple, Hyperledger.
 
However private blockchains are not true blockchains because the core philosophy of blokchain is decentralization. 

## Hybrid Blockchains

Hybrid blockchains are also controlled by an organization or a group. A level of oversight performed by the public blockchain which is required to perform certain transaction validations. Hybrid blockchains are like combination of both private and public blockchains. 

## Consortium/Federated Blockchains

 In a consortium blockchain, some aspects of the organizations/group are made public, while others remain private. A consortium blockchain is managed by more than one organisation or group so it is still semi-decentralized. There are validator nodes and member nodes in consortium blockchains. Validator nodes can validate transactions, initiate/recieve transactions. Member nodes can recieve/initiate transactions.
